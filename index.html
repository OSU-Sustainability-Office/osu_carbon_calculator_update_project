Why does sustainability even matter?

The concept of sustainability is used pretty often. We've found ourselves tuning it out. But sustainability, when you strip away all the rhetoric and politics, poses a pretty good question: Do we have an obligation to hand on to the next generation a world that will allow them to have at least as good a life as the one we have?

We can give it a try, by engaging in small changes. Even the smallest changes can make a difference. For example:

  • Giving up meat once a week yields a significant reduction in CO2 emissions
  • Buy our own re-usable water bottle and drink from the tap.
  • Buy carbon offsets to account for the high emissions generated by our airplane travel.
  • Or these changes can be things that we do: Buy our own re-usable water bottle and drink from the tap. Or buy carbon offsets â€“ they're pretty cheap â€“ to account for the high emissions generated by our airplane travel.
  • Volunteer! Plant trees, restore habitat, etc.
  • Donate to groups that work for a more sustainable future.
